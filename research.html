Research

Precision epidemiology for emerging pathogens

Targeted public health interventions require a precise understanding of infectious disease epidemiology. Our recent work has shown how tools such as the NHS COVID-19 app can be seen as effective measurement tools for precision epidemiology.

Digital pandemic preparedness

We have an extensive expertise in contact-tracing apps and more generally in epidemic apps, such as the NHS COVID-19 App deployed in England and Wales. Our aim is to design and plan for future apps and digital tools.
Programme supported by the Oxford Martin School.

Vaccine trial modelling

Modelling and designing future vaccine trials is a key resource for the 100 Days Mission for epidemic preparedness.
We are involved in the PRESTO project (PREpare using Simulated Trial Optimisation) supported by CEPI.

HIV epidemiology and evolution

We aim at understanding the determinants of HIV transmission, as well as its evolution. Research within the PANGEA consortium.

Genomics and evolution

We are interested in the genomics, phylogenetics and evolution of SARS-CoV-2 and HIV, but also in other human pathogens such as influenza A/B, as well as animal pathogens such as ASFV.

Modelling, Big Data and society

From complex networks to human interactions.
Currently analysing large-scale proxemic data and working on statistical approaches for the calibration of Bluetooth signal strength.
